Please consider the article *Debtkit: Debt Sustainability Analysis and Fiscal Risk Assessment in R* for publication in the R Journal.

The article introduces the `debtkit` package, which implements the standard debt sustainability analysis (DSA) workflow in R. Twelve exported functions cover the full toolkit: deterministic debt-path projection, historical decomposition into interest, growth, primary-balance and stock-flow-adjustment contributions, Bohn (1998) fiscal-reaction-function estimation, stochastic fan charts, the six standardised IMF stress tests, IMF-style heat maps, gross-financing-needs projection, and European Commission S1 and S2 sustainability gaps. Until now R users running a DSA have had to combine bespoke code with generic time-series packages; `debtkit` consolidates the methods used by the IMF Sovereign Risk and Debt Sustainability Framework and the EC Fiscal Sustainability Report into a single tested package on CRAN, with no dependencies beyond base R and `cli`.

Readers at the R Journal working in public economics, central banking, sovereign analysis, and fiscal policy will find an immediate use for the package. The worked case study in the paper applies the full pipeline to post-pandemic US federal debt, using FRED and World Bank data, and reproduces the main qualitative conclusion of Blanchard (2022) that US debt dynamics remain manageable at current interest-growth differentials but with a margin that is narrow and sensitive to rate assumptions.

The manuscript has not been published in a peer-reviewed journal, is not currently under review elsewhere, and all rights to submit rest with the sole author.
